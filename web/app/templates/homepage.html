Frequently Asked Questions

A Chess Bot Competition is an event where programmers create AI algorithms to play chess. These bots compete against each other in a tournament-style format to determine which AI is the strongest chess player. In this specific competition, bots are only given 5 billion instructions to make their moves.
To participate, you need to develop a chess-playing algorithm and submit it to our platform. We provide APIs and documentation to help you get started. Once submitted, your bot will be entered into the competition and ranked automatically!
The only requirement for this competition is that your bot is an x86-64 compiled, glibc compatible binary. This means you can use any programming language that can compile to a binary, including C, C++, Rust, Go, and more. Please take a look at the CPU reference for more details.
